Is there a connection between lower rates of cancer and the use of olive oil?

A Danish team of researchers from the Copenhagen University Hospital conducted a study to investigate the matter.

In the course of the study, researchers added olive oil to their daily diet for a period of two weeks.

At the end of the two week period, the study participants were found to have thirteen percent less 8oxodG in their urine, suggesting the potential for reduced levels of DNA damage (DNA damage can lead to the development of cancer) as a result of using olive oil.
